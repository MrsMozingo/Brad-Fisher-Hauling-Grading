Grading is like smoothing out the ground for a painting, making sure the surface is even and at the right angle for water to drain away.

Compacting soil is like packing down snow for a strong snowball. We make the ground firmer so things built on it won't sink or tilt.

Buried utilities are things like water pipes or cables under the ground. We need to find them to avoid accidents, like a water burst or power cut.

Changing the land's slope can be tricky because we need to make sure water still drains well and doesn't cause flooding or erosion.

Laser grading uses laser beams to measure and level the ground with superhero-like accuracy, making sure everything is perfectly flat.

Reshaping the land directs rainwater away from our homes, almost like building a tiny hill so water runs down and away, keeping everything nice and dry.
